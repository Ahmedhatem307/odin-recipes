Swedish Meatballs

Description

Swedish meatballs, or "k√∂ttbullar," are a beloved comfort food that epitomizes Swedish home cooking. These flavorful meatballs are typically made from a mixture of ground beef and pork, combined with breadcrumbs soaked in milk, finely chopped onions, and a blend of warm spices like allspice and nutmeg. The mixture is formed into small, bite-sized balls and then browned in a skillet until they develop a lovely, crispy exterior. This initial searing not only locks in the juices but also creates a rich base for the accompanying sauce.

The sauce for Swedish meatballs is a creamy, luscious gravy made by deglazing the pan with beef broth and then stirring in heavy cream, sour cream, and a touch of Dijon mustard for a bit of tang. The browned meatballs are then simmered in this sauce until they are cooked through and infused with the creamy flavors. Typically served with lingonberry jam and mashed potatoes or buttered egg noodles, Swedish meatballs offer a delightful balance of savory, sweet, and tangy tastes. This dish is perfect for a cozy family dinner or a special gathering, bringing a taste of Swedish tradition to the table.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter (for frying)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard

Steps

  1. Prepare the Breadcrumbs and Milk Mixture
  2. Mix the Meatball Ingredients
  3. Form the Meatballs
  4. Cook the Meatballs
  5. Make the Roux
  6. Deglaze the Pan
  7. Add Cream and Mustard
  8. Simmer the Meatballs in Sauce
  9. Plate and Garnish
