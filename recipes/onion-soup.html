Onion Soup Recipe

Description

Onion soup, a classic French dish known as "soupe à l'oignon," is a comforting and flavorful soup that captures the essence of simple yet hearty cooking. The foundation of this dish is the slow caramelization of onions, which brings out their natural sweetness and rich, savory flavors. Typically, yellow or white onions are thinly sliced and cooked in butter or olive oil over low heat until they become golden brown and fragrant. This process can take up to an hour but is crucial for developing the deep, complex taste that defines a good onion soup. Once the onions are caramelized, they're deglazed with white wine or sherry, and then combined with beef or vegetable broth, a bay leaf, and thyme, simmering until the flavors meld beautifully.

The soup is traditionally served with a slice of toasted baguette and a generous topping of grated Gruyère cheese, which is melted under the broiler until bubbly and golden. The combination of the sweet, caramelized onions with the robust broth, crispy bread, and gooey cheese creates a delightful mix of textures and flavors in every bite. Some variations might include a splash of brandy or the addition of garlic for extra depth. Onion soup is often enjoyed as a starter, but it can also be a satisfying main course when paired with a simple green salad and a glass of wine.

Ingredients

  • 4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to aid caramelization)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or sherry
  • 8 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves (or 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 baguette, sliced
  • 2 cups grated Gruyère cheese

Steps

  1. Prepare the Onions
  2. Caramelize the Onions
  3. Deglaze the Pot
  4. Add Broth and Herbs
  5. Prepare the Bread and Cheese
  6. Assemble and Serve
  7. Broil the Cheese
